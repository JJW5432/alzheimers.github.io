Genetic Test

This test examines the alleles of certain genes assosciated with Alzheimer's. Certain genes, such as Amyloid Precursor Protein (APP), prenesilin 1, and prenesilin 2 guarantee that a patient will develop Alzheimer's, and increase risk of early onset. However, these genes account for fewer than 1% of cases; other mutations that increase the risk but do not guarantee that Alhzeimer's will develop are far more common. The most reliable such allele is APOE ε4, but other genes such as BIN1, CLU, PICALM, and CR1 are also tested. In addition, certain alleles can offer protection from Alzheimer's: while APOE ε4 increases risk, APOE ε2 reduces it.
