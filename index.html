Alzheimer's By the time you know…

it's already too late


Once neurodegeneration occurs, the damge is irreversable, so why would you wait until there are behavioral changes to diagnose Alzheimer's?

We'd like to set the bar a little higher: to diagnose Alzheimer's before the damage affects behavior, so that we stop the disease before it affects your loved ones.

Ocular Test


For a cheap and noninvasive look at conditions inside the brain, the eye exam is a valuable first tool. We examine multiple factors that could betray brain damage, such as retinal thickness, plaques, and vascular structure.

PET Scan


A radioactive tracer that bonds to amyloid is used to image the amyloid plaques in the brain, providing a way to diagnose the progress of the disease in vivo .

Blood/CSF Test


This test measures levels of three proteins in the blood and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF). The CSF test, while more invasive, is more accurate, while the blood test is easy and cheap to administer.

Genetic Test


Certain genetic mutations greatly increase the risk of developing Alzheimer's, such as APOE ε4.
