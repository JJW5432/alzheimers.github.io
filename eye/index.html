Ocular Test

Due to its convenience, this test is administered most frequently. It examines many characteristics of the eye to detect brain damage. A thinning retina can betray neuron death and prompt more testing to determine the cause. Using a fluorescent tracer, scientists can see retinal plaques that correlate with brain plaques. Telltale changes in the veins of the eye, specifically in thickness, length, tortuosity (curviness), and branching patterns can also alert doctors to brain issues.
